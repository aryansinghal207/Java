Management - An Overview

Management is the process of planning, organizing, leading, and controlling the efforts of organization members and of using all other organizational resources to achieve stated organizational goals. The key is achieving goals efficiently (doing things right) and effectively (doing the right things).

Evolution of Management Thought

Approach Key Focus Main Contributors
Classical Efficiency, structure, rules. Treating the organization as a machine. F.W. Taylor (Scientific), H. Fayol (Administrative), M. Weber (Bureaucracy)
Neo-classical (Human Relations) Social factors, employee morale, human behavior. Elton Mayo (Hawthorne Studies)
Modern Organization as a system, adapting to the environment (Contingency Approach). Katz, Kahn, Fiedler

What are the key Managerial Skills?

1. Technical Skills: Knowledge and proficiency in a specific field.

2. Human Skills: The ability to work well with other people.

3. Conceptual Skills: The ability to think and to conceptualize about abstract and complex situations.

Is Management a Science or an Art?

Click to reveal

As a Science: It has a systematized body of knowledge and universal principles.

As an Art: It requires creativity, intuition, and practice to apply knowledge effectively.

Conclusion: Management is a blend of both. It's an art based on a foundation of science.

Is Management a Profession?

Click to reveal

It has some characteristics of a profession (body of knowledge, formal training) but lacks others (mandatory ethical code, representative body like doctors/lawyers). It's on the path to becoming a full-fledged profession.

Planning - An Overview

Planning is the primary function of management. It involves defining goals, establishing strategies to achieve those goals, and developing plans to integrate and coordinate activities.

Types of Plans

  • By Breadth: Strategic, Tactical, Operational.
  • By Time Frame: Long-term, Short-term.
  • By Use: Single-Use (for one-time events), Standing (ongoing).

Decision Making

Decision making is the cognitive process of selecting a course of action from among several alternatives. It's the core of planning.

Types: Programmed (routine, repetitive) and Non-Programmed (unique, unstructured).
